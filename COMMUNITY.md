# 🌍 DigitalBloom Community Programme

> *"AI literacy is not a privilege — it is a right. No community should be left behind in the AI revolution."*

---

## What is the Community Programme?

The **DigitalBloom Community Programme** is the open-access, donor-supported arm of the DigitalBloom AI Programme. It delivers the same curriculum, the same facilitators, and the same certification as the commercial programme — but at **little or no cost** to participants.

The community programme is built on a simple belief: the communities that stand to gain the most from AI are often the ones with the least access to quality training. We are committed to changing that.

---

## Who Can Participate?

DigitalBloom's community programme is open to any group or individual where financial barriers would prevent access. Priority is given to:

| Group | Why We Prioritise Them |
|---|---|
| 🎓 **Youth & Students** | The next generation of workers must enter the AI-transformed job market ready to contribute |
| 👩 **Women & Girls** | AI is a male-dominated field — we actively work to close the gender gap in AI literacy and careers |
| ⛪ **Faith-Based Communities** | Churches, mosques, and faith networks serve millions — equipping their members multiplies reach |
| 🏘️ **Community Organisations & CBOs** | CBOs and grassroots organisations do vital work — AI tools can dramatically increase their impact |
| 🏛️ **Local Government Units** | Ward administrators, county officers, and civil servants serving communities deserve AI capacity |
| 🌾 **Rural & Peri-Urban Communities** | Geographic distance should not mean digital exclusion |
| 🧑‍💼 **Job Seekers & Informal Workers** | AI career tools can transform employment outcomes for people in job transition |
| 🏥 **Health & Social Service Workers** | Community health workers, social workers, and volunteers can serve better with AI support |
| 🔴 **Refugees & Displaced Persons** | AI skills are portable, universal, and life-changing for people rebuilding their futures |
| 🧓 **Older Adults** | Digital inclusion includes the generation that built our communities — no one should be left behind |

## What We Offer

### 🆓 Free Pilot Session (Always)

Every community group receives at least **one free pilot session** before any commitment — regardless of whether a full programme is funded. This is our FCRM (Feedback, Complaint, and Response Mechanism) promise.

The pilot is:
- A full 3-hour facilitated session
- Hands-on and practical — not a presentation
- Followed by structured feedback collection
- Used to calibrate the full programme to the community's specific needs

### 📚 Full Programme Access (Donor-Funded)

Where donor funding is available, communities can access:
- Full course series (5 sessions × 3 hours)
- Multiple cohorts within the same organisation
- Certification for all participants
- Follow-up support and resource materials

### 🌐 Flexible Delivery

We adapt to the community, not the other way around:
- **In-person** at community venues, churches, schools, or public spaces
- **Virtual** for groups with internet access
- **Hybrid** for mixed-connectivity communities
- **Weekend and evening cohorts** for working participants
- **Swahili and local language support** where available and needed

---

## The FCRM Process

Our **Feedback, Complaint, and Response Mechanism (FCRM)** ensures quality and community voice at every stage:

```
1. ENGAGE      → STL meets community leadership to understand needs and context
        ↓
2. PILOT       → Free pilot session delivered; participant feedback collected
        ↓
3. REVIEW      → STL and community review pilot feedback together
        ↓
4. DESIGN      → Programme tailored to community context, language, and priorities
        ↓
5. DELIVER     → Full programme delivered with ongoing quality checks
        ↓
6. EVALUATE    → Post-programme assessment, outcome documentation, and impact reporting
        ↓
7. SUSTAIN     → Community facilitators trained to continue delivery independently
```

## Community Programme Outcomes

Participants in DigitalBloom community sessions have demonstrated:

- **Improved employability** — AI-skilled participants are better positioned in competitive job markets
- **Enhanced organisational effectiveness** — CBOs and NGOs using AI tools report higher productivity and better documentation
- **Increased confidence** — many community participants report that AI literacy transformed how they see their own capabilities
- **Multiplier effect** — trained participants teach family members, colleagues, and community peers
- **Economic opportunity** — AI skills open freelance and remote work opportunities, particularly for youth and women
